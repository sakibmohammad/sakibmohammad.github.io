## About Me

I am an Assistant Professor of Electronics Engineering Technology at Fairmont State University. I received my Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Southern Illinois University Carbondale in 2025. Beginning in August 2026, I also serve as the Program Coordinator for Electronics Engineering Technology.

My research applies machine learning and computer vision to microscopy, cellular mechanobiology, materials data, and embedded systems. My teaching includes circuit analysis, electronics, digital systems, microcontrollers, robotics, and senior design.

I also participate in undergraduate research mentoring, curriculum development, ABET assessment, institutional service, and STEM outreach.

## Recent Work

<div class="entry-card">

### Agentic AI for Hydrogel Data

Development of an agent-based framework for hydrogel data analysis, predictive modeling, and conditional generation.

</div>

<div class="entry-card">

### Cross-Modal Tissue Segmentation

Development of a knowledge-distillation method for single-channel tissue segmentation using information from foundation models.

</div>

<div class="entry-card">

### Microscopy Image Upsampling

Evaluation of interpolation and deep-learning upsampling methods based on their effects on downstream microscopy image classification.

</div>

<div class="entry-card">

### Embedded Object Detection

Development of a Raspberry Pi-based object-detection system for undergraduate research and search-and-rescue training applications.
